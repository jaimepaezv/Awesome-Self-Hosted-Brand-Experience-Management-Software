# Awesome Self-Hosted Brand Experience Management Software [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge.svg)](https://awesome.re)

A curated list of awesome **free as in freedom** (libre/open source) and **self-hosted** software for **Brand Experience Management (BXM)**.

> **"Free as in freedom"** means the software respects the user's freedom, as defined by the Free Software Foundation.  This generally implies the software is licensed under an OSI-approved license like GPL, MIT, Apache, etc.
>
> **"Self-hosted"** means you can install and run the software on your own servers or infrastructure, giving you control over your data, brand perception, and customer interactions.
>
> **"Brand Experience Management (BXM)"** encompasses the strategies and tools used to shape and optimize every interaction a customer has with a brand, aiming to create positive, consistent, and memorable experiences that build loyalty and advocacy.

This list focuses on software that helps manage and enhance the holistic brand experience across various touchpoints. It includes tools for customer feedback, community engagement, reputation management (to the extent possible with self-hosted FOSS), and related areas that contribute to a strong and positive brand image.

## Customer Feedback & Surveys

* **LimeSurvey** - Powerful open-source survey tool.  Extensive question types, survey logic, branching, and reporting features. Ideal for gathering detailed customer feedback to understand brand perception and experience.
    * **License:** [GPLv2](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-2.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://www.limesurvey.org/](https://www.limesurvey.org/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/LimeSurvey/LimeSurvey)
    * **Notes:** Mature and feature-rich. Excellent for in-depth brand experience research and analysis.
* **Fider** - Open-source customer feedback platform focused on feature requests and ideas. Allows customers to submit and vote, providing direct insight into customer needs and brand perception.
    * **License:** [MIT License](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)
    * **Website:** [https://fider.io/](https://fider.io/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/getfider/fider)
    * **Notes:**  Great for product-focused brands to gather feedback and build community around feature development.
* **OhMyForm** - Open-source form builder.  Simple and versatile for creating various feedback forms, contact forms, and quick surveys to gauge customer sentiment.
    * **License:** [MIT License](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)
    * **Website:** [https://ohmyform.com/](https://ohmyform.com/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/ohmyform/ohmyform)
    * **Notes:** Lightweight and easy to use for embedding feedback forms on websites and brand touchpoints.
* **Nextcloud Forms** (Nextcloud App) -  Forms app for Nextcloud. If you are already using Nextcloud, this provides a simple way to create surveys and collect feedback within your ecosystem.
    * **License:** [AGPLv3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.en.html)
    * **Website & Source Code:** [Nextcloud App Store](https://apps.nextcloud.com/apps/forms) (Source code within the Nextcloud ecosystem)
    * **Notes:** Integrates seamlessly with Nextcloud. Good for internal feedback or for brands heavily invested in the Nextcloud ecosystem.

## Community Building & Forums

* **Discourse** - Modern open-source forum platform. Excellent for building online communities around your brand. Offers rich features, engagement tools, and strong moderation capabilities.
    * **License:** [GPLv2](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-2.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://www.discourse.org/](https://www.discourse.org/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/discourse/discourse)
    * **Notes:** Highly popular and feature-rich forum software. Strong for fostering brand loyalty and customer-to-customer support.
* **Flarum** -  Simple and fast forum software. Focuses on a clean and modern user experience, suitable for building lightweight brand communities.
    * **License:** [MIT License](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)
    * **Website:** [https://flarum.org/](https://flarum.org/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/flarum/core)
    * **Notes:**  Easy to set up and use. Good for brands looking for a less complex forum solution.
* **Lemmy** - Open-source link aggregator and forum, similar to Reddit. Can be used to create a community forum around your brand or industry.
    * **License:** [AGPLv3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://join-lemmy.org/](https://join-lemmy.org/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/LemmyNet/lemmy)
    * **Notes:** Decentralized and federated, offering a different approach to community building.
* **Pebble** - Lightweight, minimalist blog and forum engine. Can be adapted for small brand communities or discussion areas.
    * **License:** [Apache License 2.0](https://www.apache.org/licenses/LICENSE-2.0)
    * **Website:** [https://getpebble.io/](https://getpebble.io/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/pebble-dev/pebble)
    * **Notes:** Very simple and fast. Suitable for smaller, focused brand discussions.
* **Friendica** - Decentralized social network platform. Can be used to create a brand community with social networking features beyond just forums.
    * **License:** [AGPLv3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://friendi.ca/](https://friendi.ca/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/friendica/friendica)
    * **Notes:** Offers a broader social networking experience for community members.

## Reputation Monitoring & Social Listening (Building Blocks)

* **Nitter** -  Free and open-source alternative front-end to Twitter. Can be self-hosted to monitor public Twitter feeds related to your brand without directly using the official Twitter platform. *Primarily for viewing, not full social listening.*
    * **License:** [GPLv3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://nitter.net/](https://nitter.net/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/zedeus/nitter)
    * **Notes:**  Limited social listening capabilities but can be a building block for monitoring public Twitter mentions.  *Terms of service of platforms need to be considered when scraping or monitoring.*
* **RSS Readers (Self-Hosted)** - Tools like [FreshRSS](https://freshrss.org/), [Tiny Tiny RSS](https://tt-rss.org/), or [Miniflux](https://miniflux.app/) can be used to aggregate news and blog feeds that mention your brand or related keywords.
    * **License:** Varies (often GPL or MIT)
    * **Examples:** [FreshRSS (GPLv3)](https://github.com/FreshRSS/FreshRSS), [Tiny Tiny RSS (GPLv3)](https://tt-rss.org/), [Miniflux (Apache 2.0)](https://miniflux.app/)
    * **Notes:**  Basic reputation monitoring by tracking mentions in publicly available feeds. Requires setting up relevant feeds and keyword monitoring.
* **Web Crawlers & Scraping Libraries (Python - Scrapy, Beautiful Soup; Node.js - Cheerio, Puppeteer)** -  Build custom scripts to crawl and scrape websites, forums, and public social media (respecting terms of service) for brand mentions and sentiment analysis.
    * **License:** Libraries are typically MIT, Apache, or similar.
    * **Notes:** Requires significant technical skills but provides maximum flexibility for building tailored reputation monitoring solutions. *Ethical considerations and platform terms of service are paramount when web scraping.*

**Important Note on Reputation Monitoring:**  Truly comprehensive, real-time social listening and reputation management often relies on access to platform APIs and large datasets, which are typically offered by commercial services.  Open-source self-hosted options in this area are more likely to be building blocks or limited in scope.  Consider the ethical and legal implications of web scraping and platform terms of service when building custom solutions.

## Brand Asset Management (BAM)

* **ResourceSpace** -  Digital Asset Management (DAM) system that can be used for Brand Asset Management. Organize, manage, and share brand assets like logos, images, videos, and guidelines, ensuring brand consistency.
    * **License:** [GPLv3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://www.resourcespace.com/](https://www.resourcespace.com/)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub](https://github.com/resourcespace/ResourceSpace)
    * **Notes:** Mature DAM platform with a strong focus on asset organization and sharing.
* **Pimcore (Community Edition)** - Open-source platform for Product Information Management (PIM), Master Data Management (MDM), and Digital Asset Management (DAM). Can be used to manage brand assets alongside product data.
    * **License:** [GPLv3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.en.html)
    * **Website:** [https://www.pimcore.com/en](https://www.pimcore.com/en)
    * **Source Code:** [GitHub (link on website)](https://www.pimcore.com/en)
    * **Notes:**  Comprehensive platform, potentially more complex to set up than dedicated DAMs but offers broader data management capabilities.
* **Bynder (Local Docker Version - License Required)** - While Bynder is primarily a commercial DAM, they offer a local Docker version of their platform. This is *not* fully open-source "free as in freedom," but is included as a *self-hosted* option for those considering Bynder and wanting more control.  *License required, verify terms.*
    * **License:** Commercial (Local Docker Version Available)
    * **Website:** [https://www.bynder.com/en/](https://www.bynder.com/en/)
    * **Notes:**  Leading commercial DAM with a self-hosted option. *License is not FOSS, included for self-hosting consideration.* *Verify licensing details.*

**Note on Brand Asset Management:**  While true Brand Asset Management often involves workflows, approval processes, and integrations with marketing tools, these open-source DAMs provide a strong foundation for organizing and managing brand assets.

## Customer Review Platforms (Self-Hosted)

* **(Limited - Consider Building Custom Solutions):**  Directly self-hosting a full-fledged public customer review platform with all the features of major review sites is complex and may require significant development effort.

* **Possible Approaches:**
    * **Integrate Reviews into your Website/CMS:** Use CMS plugins or custom development to add review functionality directly to your website.
    * **Utilize Forum/Community Platforms (like Discourse/Flarum):** Create dedicated categories or forums within your community platform for customer reviews and testimonials.
    * **Develop a Simple Review Submission Form (using OhMyForm/Nextcloud Forms):**  Create a basic form for customers to submit reviews, which can then be moderated and displayed on your site.

**Note on Customer Review Platforms:**  Building a self-hosted, public-facing review platform that competes with established sites is a significant undertaking.  Focus on integrating review capabilities into your existing brand touchpoints or leveraging community platforms for review collection.

## Important Considerations

When choosing and using self-hosted brand experience management software, remember:

* **Define Your BXM Goals:** Clearly outline what aspects of brand experience you want to manage and improve with software.
* **Technical Expertise:** Self-hosting requires technical skills for setup, configuration, and maintenance.
* **Scalability & Resources:** Ensure the software and your infrastructure can scale as your brand and customer base grow.
* **Data Privacy & Security:** You are responsible for protecting customer data and ensuring the security of your self-hosted systems.
* **Community Support:**  Active communities and good documentation are vital for self-hosted open-source software.
* **Integration:** Consider how the software integrates with your existing marketing and customer service tools.
* **Realism for Reputation Monitoring:** Open-source self-hosted reputation monitoring is often limited compared to commercial solutions. Focus on building blocks and realistic expectations.
